Silver bromide, AgBr

Solubility in water

Insoluble salt

Color

pale-yellow Solid [  ]

Molar mass

187.77 g/mol

Preparation

prepared by the reaction of silver nitrate with an alkali bromide, typically potassium bromide:

AgNO3(aq) + KBr(aq) → AgBr(s)+ KNO3(aq)

Although less convenient, the salt can also be prepared directly from its elements.

Melting point

432 °C

Boiling point

1,502 °C

Conductivity

Conduct electric in molten stage

Other

a soft, pale-yellow, water-insoluble salt well known (along with other silver halides) for its unusual sensitivity to light.

Photosensitive

widely used in photographic films and is believed by some to have been used for making the Shroud of Turin.
