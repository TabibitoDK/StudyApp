Electrochemical series

Electrochemical series is a series of metal arrangements according to the standard electrode potential, E0 in the order of most negative to most positive.

More electropositive metals are stronger reducing agents because the E0 value is more negative; so, atoms are easier to lose electrons.

More electropositive metals are located at the higher position in the electrochemical series.

The reaction that takes place involves the transfer of electrons from metal atom to metal ion.

The more electropositive the metal, the easier it is for the metal atom to release electrons.

Metals at the top of the electrochemical series are the stronger reducing agents.

In contrast, the lower the position of the metal ions in the electrochemical series, the easier it is for the metal ions to receive electrons.

Metal ions that are located at the bottom of the electrochemical series are the stronger oxidising agents.
