Waves

wave is a kind of oscillation (disturbance) that travels through space and matter.

Wave propagation transfer energy from one place to another without transforming the matter of the medium

Progressive wave

Wave profile propagates with time along the direction of propagation of the wave

Examples

Water waves, sound waves, light waves, radio waves, S-waves in earthquake, P-waves in earthquake

 

Stationary wave

Wave profile does not propagate with time

Examples

Produced by musical instrument: guitar, drum, ukulele, flute

 

Types of waves

Mechanical waves

Electromagnetic waves

Requires medium to transfer energy form one point to another

Made up of vibrating particles of medium

Does not require medium to transfer energy

Made up of oscillating electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to one another

Example

Example

Water waves, sound waves, seismic waves on the surface of the Earth

 

Radio waves, light waves and gamma rays

 

Types of progressive waves

Longitudinal waves

Particles of the medium vibrate in the direction parallel to the direction of propagation of the wave.

Made up of consecutive compressions (compressed regions) and rarefactions (stretched regions).

Examples

Sound waves

 

Transverse waves

Particles of the medium vibrate in the direction perpendicular to the direction of propagation of the wave.

Made up of consecutive crests and troughs.

Examples

Radio waves, water waves, light waves
