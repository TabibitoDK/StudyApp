Reflection of waves

Reflection is the change in direction of a wavefront at an interface between two different media so that the wavefront returns into the medium from which it originated

Can be showed in ripple tank

Law of reflection

for specular reflection (mirror)

the angle at which the wave is incident (i) on the surface equals the angle at which it is reflected (r)

 

Characteristics of wave

Effect after reflection of wave

Angle of incident, i and angle of reflection, r

i = r

Wavelength

No change

Frequency

No change

Wave speed

No change

Direction of propagation

Change with the condition that the incident angle is same as the reflection angle, i = r

Not always perpendicular

 

 

Applications of reflection of waves

SONAR technology

Technology of ultrasonic reflection which is known as SONAR helps to detect areas which have a lot of fish.

Transducer transmits waves into the water and these waves are reflected by the fish to the transducer.

Examine a foetus and other organs in medical field

Ultrasonic waves are used in the medical field to examine a foetus or other internal organs.

Identify structure of seabed / natural gas source location

Patterns of reflected sound waves caused by different rocks enable the location, depth and structure of the seabed which contains sources of natural gas to be identified.

Antenna (ASTRO)

Radio waves from communication satellites are reflected by the parabolic dish and focused onto the antenna on the feed horn.
